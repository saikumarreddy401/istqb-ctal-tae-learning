## Scenario 1 — Regression justification
Situation: Braking ECU, 3-week manual regression,
4 engineers, 6-month TAF build.
Question: Why is automation justified despite high cost?
Answer: B — High regression frequency recovers investment
Wrong traps: A (no elimination of all defects),
C (engineers not permanently freed), D (complexity ≠ thoroughness)

---

## Scenario 2 — Rapidly changing UI
Situation: Infotainment UI changes every 2 weeks.
Question: Which disadvantage is most relevant?
Answer: B — Rigidity and less adaptability to SUT changes
Wrong traps: A (cost is real but not most relevant here)

---

## Scenario 3 — ABS tests pass after behavior change
Situation: 40% of automated ABS tests pass after SW update
despite suspected behavioral change.
Question: Which limitation explains this?
Answer: C — Automation only verifies what it is programmed
to check. Test oracle was not updated.

---

## Scenario 4 — Fewer defects = better quality?
Situation: Manager claims fewer defects found = improved quality.
Question: Best critique?
Answer: B — Fewer defects found does not mean fewer exist.
Automation only checks programmed behavior.
